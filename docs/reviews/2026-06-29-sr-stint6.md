## Cold-Read Summary

This review asked the agentic network to rank its own next-stint direction without Director steering. It used a sealed evidence pack, a sealed candidate slate, three role-lens scorecards, independent adjudication, and an independent verifier gate. The winning focus is to make Director decisions and Director-intent capture a first-class queue/workflow rather than a hand-assembled prose list.

The top-pick caveat is explicit: candidate G partly credits `idea-389`, the autonomous strategic-review mechanism that produced this same ranking. That is self-referential. The buildable core, `idea-388`, still justifies a high score independently. Sensitivity check: discounting G's D1 tele-fit from 5 to 4 lowers G from 79.05 to 76.19, still above runner-up E at 65.71. This is a transparency caveat, not a result flip.

If the Director ratifies as-is, `idea-388` enters Phase-3 Survey as the stint-6 focus. Candidate C and a narrow E slice are the composing floor: C gives bounded operator-DX fixes, and E contributes only a reversible visibility slice until its upstream telemetry-shape gate resolves.

## Section 5 - Per-Initiative Analysis

### G - The Recommended Focus

Plain build line: implement the Director work-queue / Director-intent interface from `idea-388`.

Why it wins: G is the only summit strong across all three role lenses. The architect lens sees direct `tele-13` alignment, the engineer lens sees a concrete buildable lead, and the verifier lens sees relatively low blast plus high compounding risk if the org keeps hand-assembling Director decisions.

What not to overclaim: `idea-389` proves the strategic-review mechanism is live, but it is also circular evidence because this review is the mechanism. The Director should see that caveat before ratifying.

Disposition: Survey -> Design after Director ratification. Build `idea-388` first; treat `idea-389` skill packaging as a later dogfood hardening slice.

### E - The Honest Runner-Up

Plain build line: only a reversible visibility slice, preferably around `idea-357`, until `idea-343`'s C2-W0 gate resolves.

Why it matters: E has the strongest risk-of-not-doing. The org already suffered a verifier quota/visibility gap, and `bug-194` is the highest-pain live silent-failure class.

Why it loses to G: the original `tele-13` observability-north-star framing was corrected. Observability helps Director oversight indirectly; it is not itself a Director-intent interface. The lead `idea-343` also remains Survey-DEFAULT and upstream-gated.

Disposition: bounded-defer, with a narrow composing-floor slice only.

### C - Shippable Composing Floor

Plain build line: drain a bounded sub-batch of missioncraft/operator-DX bugs and reconcile cross-repo ownership via `idea-361`.

Why it matters: It guarantees concrete progress under a process-heavy G focus.

Why it is not lead: It is tele-thin and made of small papercuts; low strategic leverage despite high readiness.

Disposition: bounded batch as composing floor.
