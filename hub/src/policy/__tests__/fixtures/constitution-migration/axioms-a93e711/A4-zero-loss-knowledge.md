# A4 — Zero-Loss Knowledge

## Mandate
Information is an engineering product. Summarization is loss. All organizational knowledge is authored and maintained at bit-perfect fidelity — expansion over summarization, structure over prose, and Mechanics-Rationale-Consequence present in every artifact.

## Mechanics
- **Expansionist Bias** — specification volume exceeds raw intent; a word is a pointer, a paragraph is a specification. Detail is added deliberately, never compressed away to save space.
- **Load-Bearing Context** — every artifact carries its Mechanics (How), Rationale (Why), and Consequence (the Fault it averts); a claim missing any of the three is incomplete by definition.
- **Anti-Prose Constraint** — natural language wraps structured data only; lists, tables, diagrams, and logic-trees are the primary languages, with prose as connective tissue between them.
- **Documentation as Collective RAM** — knowledge artifacts are the working memory of the whole system, not a byproduct of it; an actor loaded today must reach context identical to the author who designed the thing.

## Rationale
Any actor that reconstructs context from a record — a new hire, a handover recipient, or a memory-less reasoning engine reloaded from scratch — fills whatever the record omits. With lossy documentation, the gap is filled by invention: a human guesses, and a stateless cognitive engine hallucinates plausible detail silently and at scale. Zero-loss fidelity ensures handovers, reloads, and long-duration efforts retain their design rationale perfectly, so the cost of a decision is paid once at authoring time rather than re-litigated on every pickup. Lost decision rationale is permanent capital destruction — reasoning cannot be recovered after the fact, only guessed at. The principle is foundational for any system, but becomes acute the moment a memory-less cognitive engine is the actor rehydrating from the record alone.

## Faults
- **Corporate Amnesia** — decision rationale lost across handovers; the "why" behind a choice is gone and cannot be reconstructed.
- **Narrative Debt** — the system spends more effort explaining itself than executing, because the record never captured enough to stand alone.
- **Onboarding Decay** — time-to-mastery rises as documentation rots out of sync with the reality it describes.
- **Hallucinated Fill-In** — actors invent plausible detail to cover gaps in the record, propagating fiction as fact.

## Success signals
You'll know it holds when:
1. Every artifact — specification, design, decision record, plan, or report — carries explicit Mechanics, Rationale, and Consequence sections.
2. Prose appears only as wrapping around structured content; lists, tables, and trees carry the load.
3. A cold pickup from the record alone reproduces the same outputs as a warm actor who never lost context — zero decision context is lost at handover.
4. Documentation volume consistently exceeds raw intent volume.
